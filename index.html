False Certification Discharge

An administrative federal student loan discharge available where a school falsely certified a borrower's eligibility for federal student aid. Educational reference, not legal advice.

What this discharge is

The False Certification Discharge program provides federal student loan relief where a school falsely certified the borrower's eligibility to receive federal student aid. Like other administrative discharge paths, it does not require a bankruptcy filing and is granted by the U.S. Department of Education.

The program covers Direct Loans, FFEL Program loans, and Perkins Loans, but not private student loans.

Eligibility

Three primary categories

  1. Ability-to-Benefit (ATB) false certification: The school enrolled the borrower in a program that required a high school diploma or its equivalent when the borrower lacked one, and the school did not properly administer the required ability-to-benefit testing or alternatives.
  2. Disqualifying status: The school certified the borrower's eligibility despite a disqualifying condition (for example, a physical or mental condition, age, criminal record, or other factor that legally barred the borrower from working in the field for which the school was training them).
  3. Identity theft / unauthorized signature: The borrower's signature on the loan application or promissory note was forged, or the loan was obtained through identity theft and the borrower never received the loan benefit.

Application process

Apply through the borrower's federal loan servicer using the Department of Education's False Certification Discharge application appropriate to the category. The application requires documentation supporting the basis for false certification (for example, evidence of disqualifying status, or identity-theft documentation such as a police report).

If approved, the loan balance is discharged and previously made payments are typically refunded. The Department may also pursue recovery from the school in cases of institutional fraud.

How it interacts with bankruptcy

False Certification Discharge eliminates the loan obligation entirely. It is a separate path from Borrower Defense to Repayment, which addresses a wider range of school misconduct (misrepresentations beyond eligibility certification). The two programs sometimes apply to overlapping fact patterns; the Department of Education evaluates each application against the relevant program criteria.

Borrowers may also have parallel state-law consumer protection or fraud claims against the school itself; those are separate from federal loan discharge and pursued in different forums.

Important. This page describes the program in general terms. Eligibility determinations are fact-specific. Talk to a licensed bankruptcy attorney, a legal aid attorney, or a Department of Education ombudsman about your specific situation.
